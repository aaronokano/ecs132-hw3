\section*{Problem 3}

There were two steps involved in proving $Cov(V) = c^{2}(A'A)^{-1}$.

First, simplify $V$:

\begin{align*}
  V &= (A'A)^{-1}A'U \\
    &= A^{-1}A'^{-1}A'U \\
    &= A^{-1}U
\end{align*}

Next, plug the simplified $V$ into the $Cov(V)$ formula:

\begin{align*}
  Cov(V) &= Cov(A^{-1}U) \\
         &= A^{-1}Cov(U)A'^{-1} \\
         &= A^{-1}(c^{2}I)A'^{-1} \\
         &= c^{2}A^{-1}IA'^{-1} \\
         &= c^{2}A^{-1}A'^{-1} \\
         &= c^{2}(A'A)^{-1}
\end{align*}
